The effect of large scale military conflicts on the world's economies.

By Ali Gadzhiev and Max Rusakov

Introduction In our project, we decided to see the war's effect on the economic growth of nations. The first countries we will be analysing are Afghanistan and the United States. We will be looking into the reasons on why this conflict started as well as comparing the GDP growth of both countries. We will also include the Russia - Ukraine conflict where again we will analyse why the conflict between the nations started as well as how the GDP of both countries has been affected since the beginning of the conflict. We believe we will be able to identify similar economic patterns in different stages of the war. This will allow us to estimate future military conflicts' outcomes and build a model showing shifts in economic growth.

Afghani War History For about two decades, Afghanistan has been in a conflict with US powers since 2001 to this present day. To begin this timeline we go back to September 9, 2001, where Ahmad Shah Massoud, a commander of the Northern Alliance, an anti Taliban organisation was assassinated by al-Qaeda operatives. This has shook the status of Afghanistan. Two days later as many of us know, al Qaeda members manage to hijack four commercial planes where they later on crash into the World Trade Center. This attack has brought fear to the US citizens which made George Bush act against the organizers of this terrorist attack. This commenced the bombing of Afghanistan which has brought many losses to the nation. However, the organiser Osama bin Laden was still on the loose which continued the western invasion on Afghanistan. After 10 years of battle, Osama bin Laden was finally killed in Pakistan. Once Obama became president in 2008, he began the withdrawal of Afghani troops. This however did not end the conflict, instead, there were increases in the amount of Al Qaeda followers. Violence in Kabul has only increased in the capital. After years of the conflict, President Joe Biden decides to pull out all the US troops out of Afghanistan which has caused a complete Taliban takeover in the country. To see how much Afghanistan has suffered over the two decades we will form a comparison between the GDP of Afghanistan and the USA during the conflict. We managed to find data on Afghanistan's GDP since 2001:

Afghanistan Effects Analysis Using the data that we have discovered on the USA and Afghanistan we can make some points clear. We can see that Afghanistans GDP has suffered their biggest GDP decline in 2001. There are following reasons we believe this happened. One of the clear main reasons for such a decline in GDP is due to the terrorist attack on 9/11. This caused several things such as foregn Afghani assets to be frozen, the country becoming a war zone and corrupt leaders being elected as Afghani rulers. This has caused chaos in the country. Although the country being in a warzone, Afghanistans GDP has nearly doubled in 2002. One of the reasons we believe this happened is due to Afghanistans opium exports. Afghanistan supplies around 80% of the world’s heroin which sum up to billions of dollars. Another reason Afghanistan managed to bring their GDP back up is from support from international organizations such as the UN who attempted to put Afghanistan back on its feet. A total of 1.8 Billion dollars have been sent for aid to afghanistan with hopes that the money would be spent for beneficial reasons such as building new schools and hospitals. Since 2002, Afghanistan’s GDP has been on a slow rise averaging a gain of 6.4% annually.

United States effects analysis Now lets compare that to the GDP differenced the United States faced during this conflict. We can see that the US has had a steady growth in their GDP since 2001 with only few years they have experienced a a negative growth rate. There have been no significant changes in GDP growth in the year 2001 however it did drop down to 1% compared to the previous year being about 4%. This could be due to the concern the American people had on their national safety. A lot of people were worried that an attack like that can happen any other time which caused panic within the people. The only decline in seen in the charts is in 2009, the year after the financial crisis. This has caused the GDP of the US to decrease by about 2.6%. Although the main reasons the financial crisis happened is not the interference in Afghanistan. We can assume that it had a rather small effect o USA’s GDP. The next year that USA has experienced a negative growth in their GDP is in 2020 where the growth percentage was -2.8%. This was right before president Joe Biden decided to pull the American troops out of Afghanistan in a hurry leaving billions of dollars worth of equipment behind. This affected the whole countries GDP due to american tax paying dollars basically being gifted to the taliban. The reason the US has also experienced a negative effect on the GDP is due to the fact that the US borrowed 500 Billion dollars to fund the Afghani war. This means that eventually the US will have to pay that money back which will affect the countries GDP.

Russia Ukraine Conflict Intro For our next two countries we will be talking about the Ukraine-Russia conflict. This beginning of the tension between both countries goes back to 2013 when Ukrainian president Viktor Yanukovych ordered the suspension of trade and association talks with the European Union to revive economic ties with Russia. Since then, anti-government protests in Ukraine lead to the expulsion of president Yanukovych and he eventually fled the country to get protection from Russia. Ukraine's goal of working together with the EU was seen as a threat to Russia which caused Putin to send Russian troops into certain areas of Ukraine to proceed in their military operations. In 2014, President Obama pledged to stand with Ukraine which strengthened the alliance between the West and Ukraine. This angered President Putin as he believed that the West would begin building military bases on the border of Russia which was seen as a threat. Next, Russia put the troops in Crimea, which led to that region breaking off from Ukraine and joining Russia. This event led to tremendous economic impact on Ukraine. Hundreds of thousands of people began to hate the government which led to huge protests in Maidan, the main square of Kiev. In 2014 the GDP of Ukraine went down 10.08%, which is an even bigger economic decline than the US had during the first year of the financial crisis of 2007. The next year, the Ukrainian rate of economic growth went down another 9.77%. After the tensions on Maidan calmed down, Ukrainian GDP started to recover, giving consistent healthy growth all the way until the COVID-19 pandemic, which shook the economy of the whole world. These tensions have been going back and forth until February 2022 when Russia invaded several Ukrainian territories. To this day the conflict is still ongoing. In order to understand the economic standpoint of this conflict we will now analyse the GDP of both countries and how it was affected since the beginning of the conflict.

As we can see from the results, Russia had a higher GDP throughout the graph and we can see that since 2002, Russia's GDP has been on the rise and has been at an all time high in 2013, since then however, Russia's GDP has dropped to the numbers it had in 2010. On the other hand, we can see how Ukraines GDP has had constant growth from 2001 all the way till 2009, where they experienced a drop in numbers. After 2014, Ukraine has had a crumble in the GDP after tensions rose between Russia. We can see that both countries have suffered from the rise in tensions between the two. From the years 2013-2016 both countries' GDP has been affected negatively. Now, let's look at some of the reasons why both of these countries' GDP has been affected.
